Zimbabwessa on pula kaikesta – nyt loppuivat passit ja rekisterikilvet

There is a shortage of everything in Zimbabwe - now there are passports and license plates AARON UFUMELI The government of Zimbabwe has banned the use of foreign currency in domestic payments to keep money out of the black market.
World Lännen Media 14:21 1
Daniel Wallenius, Reuters The economic crisis has plagued Zimbabwe in southern Africa since 2008. Hyperinflation collapsed at that time and the complete meltdown of the country's economy and value of money led to a shortage of foreign currency.When there is no money, there is no import. In addition to dollars, Zimbabwe has experienced a shortage of food and fuel in recent years and suffered 15-hour power cuts.

President Robert Mugabe, who ruled the country for a long time, was overthrown in 2017. His successor Emmerson Mnangagwa was expected to turn the country's economy, but otherwise. There is still no food and fuel left, now we also have passports and license plates.

{% if paywall.show %} On Wednesday morning, hundreds of people queued up a new passport in Harare's capital, but had to turn away. Passports are not currently available because the special paper and ink they need are imported goods that you can't afford at the moment.

One of the queues is Bothwell Mhashu , who says he has applied for a passport in June 2018. It was supposed to come in three months.

- They just said my passport was not ready. I have to ask again in August. This is not fair, Mhashu says. Authorities are planning to print passports at the local central bank, as there are more than 50,000 applications in the queue. This happens if the right tools are brought to the ground. Not even express passes are granted, except for some senior officials. In addition, registration plates are out of the country, as the registration authority expects from the Central Bank of Zimbabwe a foreign currency to pay to the German supplier. - We don't know when we get the shields. Maybe in September, said an official who did not want his name to the public.
